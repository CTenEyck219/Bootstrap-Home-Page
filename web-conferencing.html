Web Conferencing

Web conferencing software is invoked by all participants in a web meeting. Some technologies include software and functionality that differs for presenters and attendees. Software may run as a web browser application (often relying on Adobe Flash, Java, or WebRTC to provide the operational platform)

Features and Benefits of Web Conferencing

Author training content and deploy courses faster

Create and deploy engaging training content within days versus weeks leveraging easy-to-use, industry-leading authoring tools. Leverage existing training materials and use SCORM and AICC compliant content.

Enable mobile learning and maximize attendance

Deliver or join training session directly from a mobile device. Provide learners the freedom to access virtual classrooms from their smartphones, tablets or desktops with no client downloads. Maximize registration and attendance leveraging built-in tools for creating emails, registration pages, reminders and catalogs.

Create learning communities to foster knowledge exchange

Enable knowledge to flourish across learner communities, with user-generated content sharing workflows and interactivity around content, including rating, tagging, Q&A, commenting and discussion with peers or experts. Ensure that users can easily find and self-enroll into content that is personally relevant for them.

Deliver immersive virtual classroom experiences

Get learners involved leveraging rich multimedia, games and activities in addition to simply sharing content. Organize the flow of classes with customizable layouts, and coordinate behind the scenes with intuitive backstage tools. Instantly repeat sessions without any preparation leveraging classroom and content persistency.

Leverage analytics to measure training effectiveness

Adobe Connect offers more than a data dump of reports that must be manipulated to glean insights. Leveraging built-in Adobe Analytics technology, you gain real-time analytics and graphical interpretations of data to instantly grasp trends, successes, and problem areas that can be acted on and optimized immediately.

Manage and track both formal and informal learning

Publish resources either as mandatory assignments, or surface personally relevant content for optional consumption. Manage and track learners across groups or down to the individual for certification, and if desired, integrate with existing Learning Management Systems (LMS) or other systems of record.
